Moroccan Chicken Tagine

Chicken, Ras El Hanout, Harissa, Preserved Lemons... I could go on. Flavours that are just meant to be.

  • 2 preserved lemons, quartered and flesh removed
  • 800g boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 large white onions, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 20g coriander, chopped
  • 20g parsley, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons powdered ginger
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • Pinch of saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon harissa
  • 1 teaspoon Ras el Hanout
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 handfulls pitted green olives
  • 200ml chicken stock
  1. Marinate chicken in ground spices and olive oil
  2. Remove flesh from preserved lemons
  3. Add the lemon flesh to a bowl along with the chicken, onion, garlic, cilantro, parsley, ginger, pepper, turmeric, and salt. If using, add the saffron, ras el hanout, and harissa. Mix well.
  4. If time allows, let the chicken marinate in the refrigerator for several hours or overnight. Cook in either a tagine or in the oven. (See below for more information on both methods.)
